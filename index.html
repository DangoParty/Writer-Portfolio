About me

Introduction

I'm Mengyue Lu, a Baylor University student passionate about storytelling. My journey at Baylor was more than just an academic pursuit; it was a voyage into the vast ocean of scholarly and fictional literature. My time at Baylor allowed me to find and express my storytelling voice.

Academic Journey

At Baylor, I majored in English, diving into the depths of classic literature, contemporary works, and scholarly criticism. It allowed me to break down large and complex paragraphs and sentences to find their meanings. My minor in Creative Writing shaped my storytelling craft and gave me a deeper appreciation for a good story.

Writing Focus

My writing mainly explores the exciting and limitless realms of high fantasy, dystopian, and detective thrillers. These genres allow me to weave complex narratives that blur the lines between the real and the surreal. I take pride in my back-and-forth conversations between characters and fantastical worlds.

Writing Style and Influence

I strive to create believable dialogue within the realm of my world, with characters that are more than just two dimensions. I often use back-and-forth interactions to weave in details of the world and sprinkle details within the narration to ignore info dumps. My inspiration comes from Kugane Maruyama, the author of the famous light novel Overlord. I love how detailed his world is and try to make my stories as intricate as his.

Aspirations and Personal Interests

Looking ahead, I aim to contribute to an ever-expanding world of electronic entertainment. When not writing, I enjoy experiencing story-driven games such as Life is Strange and The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, which complements my creative imagination.
